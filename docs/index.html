Text Based RPG

Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this project is to produce a text based role playing game in order to better understand the ruby programming language, object oriented design, and also to improve programming skill in general. The role playing game with be a text based game that users interface with through a command line interface, and so the scope of the project is fairly narrow.

General Features

  1. The player should be able to “shape” their character before beginning to play the game.

    Example

    The player may chose to play as a dwarf that begins the game with certain items or characteristics.

  2. The attributes of the character they create should have an impact on game play.

    Example

    The player may not be able to sneak past enemies as quietly when playing as an orc than if they were to play the game as gnome.

  3. The player should be able to interact with a variety of items including spells, weapons, doors, etc.

    Example

    The player encounters a door and opens it up to explore the next area of the game.

  4. The player should be able to interact with computer entities that express varying levels of intelligence.

    Example

    The player barters with a shop owner while selling an item to the shop owner.

  5. The player should be able to travel between different areas in the game world.

    Example

    The player exits their house and goes outside.

  6. The player should be able to engage in combat with creatures or NPCs.

    Example

    The player gets shoved by a drunk bar patron and stabs them.

  7. The game should feature a political and religious landscape that impacts game play.

    Example

    Crossing into human territory as an orc may provoke instant reactions of violence from human NPCs towards the player.

  8. The player should be able to do greater things as their character increases in experience.

    Example

    A character with great strength may be able to move a boulder out of the way to reveal a secret passage way.

  9. The player should be able to engage in a variety of trade or crafting practices.

    Example

    The player mixes a potion together using ingredients they’ve grown.

  10. The player should be able to engage NPCs in organic conversation. NPC responses should depend on the personality of the NPC, player character race, political situation, NPC societal status, etc.

    Example

    The player asks a peasant where the lord of a land can be found. The peasant responds with directions to the castle.
